85 Seconds to Nothing

The Doomsday Clock is an art piece create by the Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists, it counts how close we are to “midnight,” that is, doomsday. Currently, the clock sits at eighty-five seconds to the end times, the closest it has ever been. Why do we care about the clock? It doesn’t show anything useful, it’s virtue signaling, the “oh golly-gee we need to do something!” device, right? That’s what I’ve heard many people say online, the “it’s X seconds to nothing” meme, nothing ever happens, correct?

As I write this, Gaza has been leveled, nothing remains. Iran is being bombed. A madman rules the United States, a quasi-fascist pedophile who wants to do everything to distract from the fact he has touched children. Ukraine has been abandoned. Sudan bleeds. Diamonds, cobalt, rare earth minerals, everything being mined by the hands of African children to power massive data centers that provide nothing useful to this world but pollution and slop. As the old order dies and the reaper comes take its soul, a portal has been opened and from it comes the monsters. I sometimes wonder that, by writing Terraferma, by making mods for Hearts of Iron, I have become the circus, the distraction of the populace.

This is not a text about Donald Trump, or AI, or Netanyahu. It isn’t even about Flávio and Lula, the two men that have been popping up telling me oh-so-dearly to vote for them. It isn’t about how “woke” is supposedly back while India, the US, and many other countries criminalize trans people. This bread-and-circuses that we live in, the Epstein dances on Instagram Reels, the chud memes, the everything, they aren’t the subject of this. It’s the clock, the damn clock.

Why do we care about the Doomsday Clock? Why do I care about the Doomsday Clock? It’s an art piece! It’s not even a clock! Time can go back on it, so why do we bother to check it? The true answer is that I don’t know. There’s a pedophile in the White House, but I care about the clock. The lead between Lula and the man who wants to criminalize my existence is narrowing, but I care about the clock. Countries bleed for the benefits of a few men, but I only care about the clock.

I speak this, I talk all about how much I hate AI, how much I want the clock to move back, but what will I do next? I will feed by work to Claude, Gemini, BingleAI or whatever, just to get a compliment, an analysis, to be treated like an actual writer, I will not protest, I will remain in my room writing. And Claude, Gemini, and Bingle will say “what a fourth wall break!” and I will continue feeding them. Is art, as Kurt said, just a custard pie being thrown from a ladder?

Am I the circus, or am I the one watching?


After It All Burns, Will You Still Be Here?

The world does not care about you. The politicians don’t care about you. If this world is truly dead, if there’s no Montreal for climate change or AI, then what is the point? What’s the point of writing in a world that does not care about art? If we all go back to fire, be that of the forests or that of the bombs, why do we strive to do anything? Looking from Brazil, I wonder when it’s our time to be the next Venezuela or Iran, to be bombed and sequestered, to lose our sovereignty, to become a colony. I wonder this as Americans are not doing anything to stop it. They scream “no kings” but do they mount the guillotines? No, they dance. They dance like clowns.

Me and my girlfriend are hundreds of kilometers apart, yet we both deal with the same woes. Southeast Asia and Latin America seem to be brethren in corruption and people; me and her are in college trying our best to deal with the world. Malaysia still grapples with its past as Brazil seems to have forgotten it.

When this world goes to its grave, after “midnight” comes for either Earth or her systems, be it by bombs or revolution, will I be here to see it? Will we even manage to fix this world in time? I don’t know.

As it all burns, my right side is smiling, my left side is numb, and I can't feel the fire in my face.


Meu Nome

Certos momentos, especialmente o que vivemos, necessitam de explicações vindas da língua nativa de uma pessoa. Pergunto a mim mesma desde 2024 uma pergunta simples: porque Emily? Um nome anglófono, nada brasileiro. Minha resposta comum é “por causa de Stardew Valley,” mas não deve ser só isso, certo? Não entendo o porque escolhi “Emily” e porque dou às minhas personagens o nome, ou variações, de Emily.

Meu nome não é “natural”, ele foi escolhido a dedo por mim mesma. Ele pode não ser natural, ordenado pela minha certidão de nascimento, RG, CPF, mas ele é meu nome. Ele pode não ser reconhecido pela minha família, que mandou Emily pro degredo para manter seu filho, mas eu ainda tenho este nome.

Penso se um dia distante ou, preferencialmente, próximo, poderei mudar os ritos legais para me acomodar. Penso se um dia poderei tomar as pílulas ou injeções necessárias para que eu seja eu. Mas também penso se meu tempo está esgotando, que a longa batalha contra o progresso travada pela extrema-direita mundial esteja perto de entrar em território brasileiro. Esta é uma eleição em que estou (metaforicamente, mas nem tanto) entre a vida e a morte. Não irei me matar, minha existência é ato de resistência.

Um dia, talvez, meu nome e eu serão uma só. Eu espero que esse dia chegue logo.


To Exist With You

I’ve been listening to Elio Mei for the better part of the last year. Something about his songs deeply interests me. Even if we have different genders, he being a guy, me being a girl, we’re both trans, maybe that’s what made the connection. But the songs that Elio writes might be what truly made me love his work, especially his work on one specific album: To Exist With You.


Before A Thousand Songs, To Exist With You was the only album that Elio had ever produced (The Bard and Promise You Won’t Scream are EPs). You don’t notice it is the first album, you think he made a thousand albums before, it is that good. But I want to muse on the name of the album and its homonymous song.


“I love to exist with you,” the chorus of the song that gives the album its name, it’s weird right? What does it mean to want to exist with someone? Does it mean marrying them? Living together? Being friends? Or just plainly to exist in the same world as them? I believe that it just means to exist with someone, that is, to enjoy the small acts of love.


But one must wonder about the opposite feeling, what if one hates to exist with you, but they are forced everyday to be reminded of your existence? If your cloud makers make pollution, if you turn towns to ruins, why would one want to exist with you? I feel like that is the thought that passes in the heads of many people of the current era about their leaders.


If you want me to want to exist with you, why would you do everything to hurt me?
